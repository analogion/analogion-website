Message from Ioannis Triantafilou:

Dear readers of Analogion,
 
I am writing to you about the Byzantine Music Project that I've been trying to get started since last July.

My friend, Manny Anastasiou, a very serious and dedicated chanter in NYC, discovered a very large collection of Byzantine Music recordings which Archon Protopsaltis Demetrios Ioannides is selling for 15,000 euros ($18,030. 30).  This collection contains about 3000 hours of material (calculated by the footage of tape on the reels he's selling) from everyone in Constantinople at that time including Nafpliotis, Priggos, Stanitsas, and many others (the others are on the list attached below)  Most recordings of the collection are of Stanitsas.
 
I also want to add that Mr. Ioannides' entire collection exists on reels.  However, Manny and I are willing to restore, edit, produce, and convert all of the files to Mp3's free of charge.  This way, all of the recordings will be easily accessible and, of equal importance, they will be preserved through the digital files. With this project, I would like to create a Byzantine Music library that will be easily accessible (on the internet, at theological schools, etc.) for the benefit of everyone interested.  I feel that these recordings are a treasure that cannot be hidden any longer and that they will help make learning to chant, especially the Traditional Patriarchal Style, much easier. 

I am looking for sponsorship for this project, sending this message to all directions.
 
I notified Mr. Ketsetzis recently concerning this project and he is also trying to help. At this point, I am willing to ask anyone or try anything to get this project started.  I know how much a library such as this would help chanters and those aspiring to learn.

Thanks and Happy New Year,
Ioannis Triantafilou
