Analytic writing of older and modern Constantinopolitan tradition

Λειτουργία του Λυκούργου Πετρίδη και την Πατριαρχική Μουσική Κιβωτό του Ιεροθέου Φιλοθείτου (πρώτη έκδοση των δυσεύρετων Κοινωνικών του Θρ. Στανίτσα, καταγραφή της προφορικής παράδοσης, επιλογή των πλέον δοκιμασμένων μαθημάτων και γραμμών με έμφαση στα παπαδικά μαθήματα, πληρότητα λειτουργικής μουσικής ύλης). Comments by John van der Hoek
Demosthenis Paikopoulos ''Pandekti Ekkl. Mousikis - Tomos 3 - Theia Leitourgia'' [Athens 2006] 593 pages. 55 Euros. Paikopoulos has had a wide experience and a leading Chanter in Greece for a long time and in particular as second domestikos with Stanitsas 1958-1962. He studied byzantine music with Stanitsas, Pringos, Karakasis. This new litourgia presents many compositions and analyses of compositions by Stanitsas, Pringos, Balasi, Navpliotis, Karakasis, Nikolaidis, and himself and several other composers. The compositions are highly analysed and isons are provided. It would seem that this book provides a valuable analysis of compositions from the Patriarchal chanters. The book comes with a CD of a divine liturgy with selections mainly in ichos 1 and 5. The CD features a true gem [my opinion] - Devte proskinisomen by p.Agathonikos Lazoglou which is in tone 2 but seems to have some of the character of the Trisagion of Avagianou with sections in distonikos. This is a book with a rich source of new material and is a worthy book to have.
